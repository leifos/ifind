Experiment Instructions

First of all, thank you for agreeing to participate in this experiment about online searching!
During this experiment we would like you to perform three different search tasks using the same search system.

Your Role

  • For this experiment, imagine that you are a newspaper reporter, where you will need to write a story on three topics. In order to accomplish this you will need to search for related information.

The Search System

  • The search system that you will use connects to a database of about 1 million newspaper articles from the New York Times, The Associated Press and Xinhua news agencies. The articles within the database range from 1999 to 2000. The search system you'll be using looks similar to a web search engine, though there might be some slight differences to the search interface.

The Search Tasks

  • For each search task, you'll be given a short description of a topic and what information would be relevant to this topic. This can be viewed by the Shot Task button at the top right-hand corner of the screen.

  • You will pose queries and look at results, just like you would in a web search engine. When viewing documents, you can select the Mark as Relevant button when you find a document relevant to the topic. There are over 100 relevant documents in the database for each topic. Your job is to find as many of these as possible during the time allocated.

Goal and Incentive

  • As previously mentioned, your goal is to find as many relevant documents as possible during the time allocated (approximately 10 minutes per task).
  • As an additional incentive the person who identifies the most relevant documents will be given a new iPod Nano

  • The experiment will take approximately one hour of your time.

Experiment Outline

Before you start searching, we will ask you a few questions about you.

We will then give you a few minutes to get comfortable with the search interface by undertaking a practice task.

You'll then move onto the three main search tasks. Before each task, you will complete a pre-task questionnaire about your knowledge of the search topic. After you complete the task, you will complete a post-task questionnaire about the search interface.
